## WHY
### The Problem Worth Solving

Sanatan Dharma is the oldest living knowledge system on earth.

It gave humanity the concept of zero. It mapped consciousness 3,000 years before neuroscience. It described the expanding universe in the Nasadiya Sukta before telescopes existed. It produced the Bhagavad Gita — a 700-verse treatise on decision-making, identity, and purpose that still outsells most modern self-help books combined.

And yet — if you open your phone right now and try to *understand* it, this is what you find:

- Wikipedia articles written with Western academic distance
- YouTube videos oscillating between saffron nationalism and New Age vagueness
- Temple apps that assume you already believe
- WhatsApp forwards mixing genuine philosophy with fabricated "facts"
- Quora threads that devolve into religious arguments within three comments

**There is no serious, beautiful, intellectually honest digital home for Sanatan Dharma.**

That is the problem.

Not a lack of content — there is infinite content. A lack of *structure*. A lack of *relationships*. A lack of a map that shows how everything connects — how Brahman relates to Atman, how Karna's tragedy illuminates Dharma's paradoxes, how the Samkhya school of philosophy predates Greek dualism by centuries, how the same questions asked in the Upanishads are the questions Camus and Nietzsche spent their lives on.

Sanatan Dharma is not a religion in the Western sense. It is a **knowledge system** — a living, branching, self-correcting network of ideas, stories, practices, and people. It deserves to be represented as one.

### The Deeper Why

There is a generation — right now, aged 18 to 35 — that is spiritually searching.

They left the religion of their parents not because they stopped believing in something larger than themselves, but because no one gave them an intellectually honest bridge to their own tradition. They found Stoicism instead of Vedanta. They found mindfulness apps instead of Dhyana. They found Jordan Peterson talking about archetypes instead of someone talking about the Puranas.

They were not lost. They were just not found.

Devlok exists to find them.

And beyond them — the devotees who practice deeply but cannot explain what they practice to their children. The diaspora kids in London and New Jersey who want to know who Hanuman is but feel embarrassed asking. The teachers who know CBSE now mandates Indian Knowledge Systems but have no digital tool to teach it well.

All of them are the same person: someone who deserves better than what currently exists.

## WHAT
### The Product

**Devlok is an interactive knowledge graph of Sanatan Dharma** — a living, community-built map where every deity, hero, sage, concept, text, and philosophical school is a node, and every relationship between them is a visible, traversable edge.

It is not an encyclopedia. Encyclopedias are flat lists.
It is not a social network. Social networks optimize for attention, not understanding.
It is not a temple app. Temple apps serve the already-converted.

Devlok is a **thinking tool** — the kind of tool that lets you start at Krishna and arrive at Advaita Vedanta by following a chain of ideas. That lets you understand why the Mahabharata is not a war story but a philosophy text. That makes the invisible architecture of one of humanity's greatest knowledge systems suddenly visible.

### What It Contains (Current State)

```
70+   Characters across Mahabharata, Ramayana, and Puranas
100+  Relationships across 5 types: family, divine, conflict, guru, alliance
5     Scripture filters: Mahabharata, Ramayana, Puranas, Vedic, All
5     Yuga timeline filters: Satya, Treta, Dvapara, Kali, All
6     Node types: Deva, Devi, Hero, Sage, Asura, Celestial
```

### What It Becomes (Vision State)

```
1,000+  Nodes: characters + concepts + texts + acharyas + darshanas
5,000+  Relationships
        Philosophical schools mapped: Advaita, Vishishtadvaita, Dvaita,
        Samkhya, Nyaya, Vaisheshika, Mimamsa, Yoga
        Living lineages: Adi Shankaracharya → Ramanujacharya →
        Madhvacharya → Vivekananda → Aurobindo
        Sacred texts as nodes: 4 Vedas, 108 Upanishads, 18 Puranas,
        Bhagavad Gita, Yoga Sutras, Brahma Sutras
        Community-contributed regional traditions
        Daily concept feed
        Cohort-based Gurukul learning circles
```

### What It Is Not

It is not a Hindu nationalist platform.
Sanatan Dharma belongs to every person who seeks truth — regardless of birth, caste, nationality, or belief. The graph makes no political claims. It maps knowledge, not ideology.

It is not a replacement for practice.
Devlok does not tell you how to pray, fast, or worship. It helps you understand — and understanding deepens practice rather than replacing it.

It is not finished.
A knowledge system that has evolved for 5,000 years cannot be captured in a final product. Devlok is permanently incomplete — and that is the point. The community adds to it forever.

## WHEN
### The Timeline and Why Now

### Why Now Is the Right Moment

**The spiritual economy is at an inflection point.**
The global wellness market is $5.6 trillion. India's spiritual tourism alone is ₹1.5 lakh crore. Sadhguru's Inner Engineering online program has 2 million+ enrollments. The demand signal is undeniable — people are willing to pay for serious engagement with inner life.

**The Seeker generation has hit peak frustration.**
Young Indians who dismissed their tradition in their teens are now in their mid-20s and early 30s reconsidering. The pandemic accelerated this. Post-COVID identity questions have not been answered by productivity apps or Netflix.

**CBSE made Indian Knowledge Systems a subject in 2023.**
For the first time, schools need digital tools to teach Vedic mathematics, Arthashastra, Ayurveda, and Indian philosophy. No serious tool exists yet. The institutional window is open.

**The technology stack is mature.**
D3.js, MongoDB, React, and the Anthropic API are all stable, well-documented, and free-to-start. The "Ask the Rishi" AI feature that would have been science fiction 3 years ago is now a weekend project.

**No serious competitor exists yet.**
This is the rarest condition in product development. It will not last long.

## HOW
### The Build Philosophy and Execution Strategy

### Technical Philosophy

**Antigravity conventions apply universally.**
Same stack. Same patterns. Same deployment. Devlok is not a new technical adventure — it is the application of proven decisions to a new domain. This matters because every hour spent debugging unfamiliar tooling is an hour not spent on the product.

```
MongoDB Atlas    → Character and Relationship data
Express          → REST API, identical middleware patterns to Antigravity
React + Vite     → Frontend, single index.css, no component libraries
D3.js v7         → Owns the SVG DOM entirely — React does not touch it
Vercel           → Frontend, free tier, SPA rewrite mandatory
Render           → Backend, free tier, keep-alive ping mandatory
Cloudflare       → DNS, Edge protection, and CDN for divine assets
Resend           → Transactional emails (Welcome, Reset, Invites)
PostHog          → Product analytics (tracking node affinity and traversal)
Pinecone         → Vector Database for the "Ask the Oracle" AI search
Stripe           → Payment infrastructure for Gurukul and Offerings
```

**The graph is the product.**
Every technical decision asks: does this make the graph better? Faster? More explorable? More accurate? If the answer is no, it does not ship.

**Mobile is not an afterthought.**
Test on phone weekly. D3 touch conflicts caught early. The Seeker is on their phone.

**The data outlasts the code.**
MongoDB schemas designed today will hold 1,000 nodes as easily as 70. Index `type`, `yuga`, `filter` on Character. Index `source` and `target` on Relationship. Do this before the first real user arrives.

---

### Community Philosophy

**Quality over volume. Always.**

One verified, well-sourced, beautifully written character entry is worth a hundred Wikipedia copy-pastes. The contribution system enforces this through the Shraddha reputation model and mandatory source citation.

**The community builds the moat.**
Once 10,000 people have contributed to the graph, it cannot be replicated by a competitor starting fresh. The data, the relationships, the cultural context — all of it is locked in community knowledge that does not exist anywhere else. This is Wikipedia's moat. It works.

**Signed contributions. No anonymity.**
Every node, every relationship, every interpretation is attributed. You are accountable for what you add to the map of Dharma. This one design decision filters out 90% of noise before it enters the system.

**The first 50 contributors are treated as co-founders.**
Personally recruited. Personally thanked. Their names permanently on the nodes they built. The culture of the community is set by the first 50 people — choose them deliberately.

---

### Growth Philosophy

**Organic first. Paid never (initially).**

The Seeker shares things that make them look thoughtful. A beautiful, intellectually serious character card shared on Twitter or Instagram is worth more than any ad. Build the sharing infrastructure before building the marketing strategy.

**Depth beats breadth in the spiritual space.**
One person who spends 45 minutes on the graph and comes back tomorrow is more valuable than 1,000 people who bounce in 30 seconds. Optimize for depth of engagement, not top-of-funnel volume. The algorithm rewards breadth. The community rewards depth. Build for the community.

**The comparison content is the growth engine.**
"Sanatan Dharma already knew this." Side-by-sides with Stoicism, quantum physics, Jungian psychology, modern leadership theory. This content is shareable across audiences that would never search for "Hindu mythology" — but will engage with "why Vedanta and String Theory are describing the same thing."

### Revenue Philosophy

**Charge for transformation, not information.**

Information on the graph is free forever. The daily concept is free. The community is free. What is paid is the *guided experience* — the Gurukul cohort where someone holds your hand through four weeks of focused exploration.

This is the yoga studio model applied to ideas. The YouTube yoga is free. The studio membership is paid. Both serve different depths of the same seeker.

**The diaspora will pay for the premium tier without hesitation.**
They already spend on cultural maintenance — language classes, music lessons, cultural camps. A ₹199/month product that helps them pass Dharma to their children is not a financial decision for them. It is an identity decision.
