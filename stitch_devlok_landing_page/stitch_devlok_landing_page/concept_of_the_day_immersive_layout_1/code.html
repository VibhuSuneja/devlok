HIRANYAGARBHA

Origin The Golden Womb

Before the stars were scattered like salt across the dark expanse, there existed only the Hiranyagarbha—the cosmic egg that floated in the causal waters of the unmanifested universe.

In the Rig Veda, the Hiranyagarbha is hailed as the source of all existence. It is not merely a physical object, but the latent potential of all consciousness, heat, and matter condensed into a singular, radiant point of amber light.

Mantra Extraction

"In the beginning was the Golden Womb, the Lord of all that is, the sole sustainer of earth and heaven."
— Rig Veda 10.121

As the shell fractured under the pressure of divine will (Sankalpa), the universe began its expansion. The upper half of the golden shell became the heavens (Akasha), while the lower half solidified into the earth (Prithvi). Between them, the breath of life—Prana—began to circulate, giving rise to the first deity, Brahma, the architect of worlds.

Philosophically, this concept mirrors the modern Big Bang theory, yet adds a layer of consciousness. The Archive suggests that we are all fragments of that original golden light, perpetually seeking to return to the unity of the womb.
